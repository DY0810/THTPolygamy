Historical Context

In the Indian subcontinent, polygyny was traditionally practiced among royal families and upper castes. Hindu epics and legends recount tales of gods and heroes with multiple wives. However, by the mid-20th century, legal reforms—such as the Hindu Marriage Act of 1955—prohibited polygamy for most communities.

Cultural Traditions & Reforms

While historical Hindu polygamy was confined mostly to elites, Muslim communities in South Asia have continued to practice regulated polygyny. The modern era has seen a significant shift, with economic and educational advancements driving a move toward monogamy for the majority of the population.
